# Studying

## Overview

This page explains how to study in Anki, covering the fundamental workflow from selecting a deck through reviewing cards.

## Decks

Study is limited to the currently selected deck and its subdecks. The deck screen displays New, Learn, and Due cards for the day.

## Study Overview

After selecting a deck, users see a screen indicating how many cards are due. Cards fall into three categories: New, Learning, and To Review. The "Study Now" button begins a session.

## Questions & Answers

Cards initially display only the question. Users reveal answers by clicking a button or pressing Space. If you can't answer within about 10 seconds, it's probably better to move on.

## Answer Buttons

Four response options are available:

- **Again** (keyboard: 1) - for incorrect answers
- **Hard** (keyboard: 2) - correct but with doubt or delay
- **Good** (keyboard: 3/Space/Enter) - correct with mental effort (most common)
- **Easy** (keyboard: 4) - correct with no effort

## Fuzz Factor

Random delays prevent cards from appearing on the same review dates.

## Editing & More

Users can edit cards, flag them, bury or suspend them, reset progress, adjust due dates, and access card statistics.

## Display Order

Cards from subdecks appear alphabetically. Users can control ordering using special characters like `-` and `~`.

## Siblings & Burying

Related cards (like front/back pairs) can be automatically hidden after answering one, preventing redundant same-day reviews.

## Keyboard Shortcuts

- Space/Enter reveals answers
- 1-4 select difficulty buttons
- `/` opens deck selection

## Falling Behind

Anki prioritizes cards waiting longest, ensuring equitable progression through backlogs.
