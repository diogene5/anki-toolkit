# Getting Help

## Asking Good Questions

Anki and its support are provided free of charge by volunteers, except for AnkiMobile. When seeking help, demonstrate respect and effort.

Before posting, try resolving the issue yourself:

- Review the getting started section and intro videos
- For bugs, follow the troubleshooting steps
- Search this manual's FAQ
- Search the forums
- Google the issue

When you do post, explain your problem clearly with specifics. Rather than vague statements like "My Anki doesn't work," provide:

- Steps you've already taken
- The specific actions triggering the problem
- Exact error messages or symptoms

This approach makes it easier for volunteers to assist you effectively.

Note: User forums require a separate login from AnkiWeb.

## Anki Desktop and AnkiWeb Support

Post questions on the [user forums](https://forums.ankiweb.net) after reviewing the above guidance.

## AnkiDroid Support

Consult [AnkiDroid's dedicated support page](https://docs.ankidroid.org/help.html).

## AnkiMobile Support

Visit [AnkiMobile's support page](https://docs.ankimobile.net/support.html).

## Private Questions

For security issues and business inquiries, submit a private ticket [here](https://anki.tenderapp.com/discussions/private). For general Anki, AnkiWeb, or AnkiDroid questions, use the [user forums](https://forums.ankiweb.net) instead.
