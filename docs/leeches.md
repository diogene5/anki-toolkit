# Leeches

## Overview

Leeches are flashcards that users consistently fail to remember. These cards demand excessive review time compared to other material in a deck.

## How Anki Identifies Leeches

Anki tracks when review cards "lapse" (fail during review mode). Once a card lapses 8 times, the system tags the note as a leech and suspends it automatically. Users can customize this threshold and suspension behavior through deck options.

Periodic warnings continue for problematic cards at intervals equal to half the initial threshold. For instance, with a threshold of 8, subsequent alerts appear at 12, 16, and higher lapses.

## Handling Strategies

### Editing

The recommended approach involves reformulating how information is presented. Cards may contain excessive content or cover material without sufficient understanding. The documentation references "20 rules of formulating knowledge," developed by Dr Piotr Wozniak of SuperMemo, as guidance. Mnemonics, additional related cards, and improved coherence often strengthen retention.

### Deleting

When material lacks sufficient importance relative to study time invested, deletion is appropriate. Removing difficult, obscure items allows focus on more valuable content. Alternatively, cards can remain suspended indefinitely.

### Waiting

"Interference" causes some leeches — when similar concepts confuse learners. Strategic suspension of one related item until another is firmly learned can resolve this issue.
